1. Introduction to the Interactive Quizz System

Let's start with a few general remarks about the IQS.

Menu-Driven Interface

As you will quickly notice, this program is menu-driven: this means that you don't have to worry about quirky key combinations. All you have to do is click the mouse in a window to see the appropriate menu appear. Click again on one of the choices and that's it!

Try it! Don't you love it already? Sure you do!

Authentication System
The first thing to do is to make sure that you're allowed to run the program. You will be prompted for your full name and password. The program will check them and clear you if they're correct. Indeed, we don't want people to play around with each other's data.
Getting Started: Once you're clear, a window will open and you'll be ready to go to work. Have fun!

2. Student's Section

If we did things right (and I think we did), you should see this window:

Student Interface Layout

The left-hand window lists the numbers of the available quizzes. If you click on one of them, you will see a short description appear in the right-hand window. See how simple it is?

Let's now examine the available options. Click anywhere in the right-hand window.

Available Student Options

Take quiz
Selecting it starts the exam. The questions are successively printed in the right-hand window and you will be prompted for the correct answer. Remember: you have a limited time to answer the question, so don't waste it! When the quiz is over, your score is stored for later use.
Grades
You'll probably be eager to know how well you've done. That's what this option is for. Click on it and the following statistics will be displayed in the right-hand window:
  • your grade and your rank among the students who took the quiz
  • the time you spent answering the quiz, and your rank
  • the average score
  • the average time
Change password
This one is pretty obvious: it lets you change your password.
Student Tip: That's it! Good luck!

3. Teacher's Section

Hi, teach! I mean, good morning Sir!

Teacher Interface

That's how things should look on your screen once you clicked in the right-hand window (if they don't, well, why don't you have a beer: it won't help much but it'll cool you down).

In the right-hand window are displayed the number of the quizzes you've written so far. If you click on one of them, you'll see a short description appear in the right-hand window.

Available Teacher Options

Add quiz
Creates a new quiz. You'll see its number appear after the other numbers.
Remove quiz
Deletes permanently the selected quiz. Use with caution!
Edit quiz
Lets you modify a quiz.
Publish quiz
Makes a quiz available to all your students. You may want to publish a quiz once you've finished writing it.
Grades
Will print in the right-hand window the following statistics:
  • the list of the students who took the quiz, sorted by descending score
  • the average score
  • the same list, sorted by ascending time
  • the average time
Password
Enables you to change your password.

Editing Quizzes

If you selected Edit quiz, you should see this:

Quiz Editor Interface

As usual, the right-hand window's purpose is to display information about the selected item. The upper left-hand window lists the questions. The lower left-hand window lists the possible answers corresponding to the selected question.

Click in any of the left-hand window. A menu will appear. It'll give you three options:

  • Add creates a new item
  • Remove deletes permanently the selected item. Again, use with caution!
  • Edit lets you modify the selected item

Question Structure

A question is made of:

  • the question itself: Complete the sentence: My _____ is rich.
  • the score granted if the answer is right, wrong or if the student doesn't answer in time
  • the time given to the student to answer the question

Answer Structure

A possible answer is made of:

  • the answer itself: tailor
  • its validity: true (or false)
Teacher Tip: You're now ready to start writing quizzes. Please, don't be too hard on your students!

4. Sysop's Section

As Sysop, you are responsible for the administration of the program. You are the only one allowed to add or remove students, teachers, etc.

Important: You also are a teacher, which means that the Teacher's Section applies to you. We suggest that you read it carefully before reading any further.

Sysop Interface

After you logged in, you will see this:

The first option is nothing really new: it just opens a Teacher window, just like the one we just studied. Please refer to the Teacher's Section for more details.

The last option isn't new either: Password lets you change your password.

Administration Panel

Let's study the option Administration. Click on it and here we go:

Administration Interface Layout

  • Upper-left window: lists the available school years
  • Lower-left window: lists the students who are attending the selected year
  • Upper-right window: lists the teachers who are teaching to these students
  • Lower-right window: displays information about the selected item

Administration Options

Click in any of the list windows and a menu will appear, giving you three options:

Add
Creates a new item.
Remove
Deletes the selected item permanently (Have I said this before?)
Edit
Lets you edit the selected item. That's the item's name, plus its password if it's a student or a teacher.
Sysop Responsibility: Well, you know all there is to know. Remember: you are in charge. Try to be worthy!
